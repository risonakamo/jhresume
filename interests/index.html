Web Development

The web environment is one of my favourite places to program on.

I find that I code best when I am doing it for fun, and when I really have a desire to get something done, I'll do it.

The web really lets me program in a practical and artistic way, two things that I prioritise highly while writing.

I love to stare at the developer console - when I see an interesting website I will often spend way too much time trying to figure out how it works

I am always happy to see a web technology used in a way I'd never thought of before - the more I see the more I can do!

Gaming

As an avid gamer I spend a lot of time playing games.

I like to expose myself to a wide variety of genres of games on all sorts of platforms, from mobile to web-based to engine-based.

I have aspirations to become a game developer or net-game developer, I hope that one day I can become a contributor to one of the games I have played.

My experience with games is a big factor in my artistic expression; I have many references to draw from.

Computer Science

Before I entered the Computer Science field, I was in the Engineering field for the majority of high school.

This lead me to have more of a practical mentality when approaching computer science problems. I like to solve things using light and efficient methods that work for general cases.

I welcome access to new software, learning to use a new language or program is always an interesting challenge. To this extent I have used an assortment of different tools and software throughtout my education experience.

Although high-level languages like Javascript and Python are some of my favourite practical languages, I have worked with and am not uncomfortable around languages such as C or assembly, and I am always ready to learn more.
